Heart Disease Predication with Logistic Regression and Random Forest

May 11, 2023

Outline: This project focuses on predicting heart disease using machine learning models, specifically Logistic Regression and Random Forest. It begins with an introduction to the significance of healthcare data in transforming the medical industry, reducing waiting times, and improving patient care. The research integrates four heart disease datasets and explores feature selection techniques to enhance model performance. Practical application development is emphasized, where users can input their medical data for a heart disease prediction using the best-performing model. Feature selection methods such as Heatmap with Correlation, Recursive Feature Elimination, Feature Importance with Random Forest, and Univariate Selection are discussed. The preprocessing steps, including handling missing values, are explained, and the motivation for selecting Logistic Regression and Random Forest models is outlined. Hyperparameter tuning with Grid Search is performed, and model performance is evaluated in terms of accuracy, precision, recall, and F1-score. The best-performing model is determined, and the project concludes by highlighting the potential of machine learning models in healthcare and addressing challenges in providing medical advice through AI.

Programming Language: Python

Topics: Machine Learning in Healthcare, Heart Disease, Logistic Regression, Random Forest, Ensemble Learning, Feature Selection, User Interface

Brief Conclusion: This research underscores the promising potential of applying Machine Learning models to healthcare data for the accurate prediction of heart disease. Employing Logistic Regression, Random Forest, and Gradient Boosting models, we successfully combined data from four distinct datasets and achieved an overall test accuracy of 80%, with Random Forest emerging as the most proficient model. Furthermore, our investigation initiated a pathway toward enhancing predictive performance by exploring the unprocessed dataset containing 76 attributes. Four feature selection techniques, including Heatmap with Correlation, Recursive Feature Elimination, Feature Importance with Random Forest, and Univariate Selection, identified Chest Pain Type, Exercise-Induced Angina, and Max Heart Rate as the pivotal predictors of heart disease, consistent across all methods. Importantly, we emphasized the translation of this research into practical applications through the development of a user-friendly interface, enabling early heart disease detection and demonstrating the tangible potential of prediction models in healthcare settings. However, it is vital to recognize the challenges and complexities inherent in delivering medical advice via AI, and further clinical validation is essential to harness the full capabilities of these models in the field of medicine.
