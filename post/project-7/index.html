Shiny Dashboard and Exploring Monte Carlo Simulations

December 23, 2022

Shiny Dashboard: In this section, I developed an interactive Shiny app that facilitates data exploration and visualization or car data. The Shiny app allows users to choose any combination of two variables from the dataset and generates different types of plots based on the variable types. For numeric variables, a scatterplot is displayed, for a combination of a categorical variable and a numerical variable, a boxplot is shown, and for two categorical variables, a barplot or count plot is generated. The app also provides options to display a best-fit regression line along with the equation and coefficient of determination for scatterplots. Users can further customize the plots by selecting different factor variables for color differentiation. The Shiny app also features tabs for dataset summary details and histograms for the chosen variables, keeping users informed about the displayed content.

Two regression models were employed, one being the “reduced model,” which represents the best-fitted model, and the other using a combination of chosen independent variables. Brief Conclusion: Monte Carlo Simulations have proven to be a powerful tool for predicting coefficients and Standard Error values in statistical models. Our simulations closely resembled real-world data, underscoring their reliability. We also observed that increasing the number of simulations improved accuracy, but this comes at the cost of additional time and computational resources. Ultimately, Monte Carlo Simulations provide a robust method for estimating parameters in complex datasets, enhancing statistical analysis quality and insight generation.
